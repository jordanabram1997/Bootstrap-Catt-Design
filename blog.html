Why make your website responsive?

by Jordan Abram

Posted on August 19, 2014 at 8:19 PM

Web Responsivness is a must when building a website. So today I am bringing you the reasons to justify why a website must be Responsive.

My first reason to justify why web responsiveness is a must is that you need to reach out to the maximum viewers that you can get. For example some viewers may not have a computer. Therfore your website will not look the best on a smart device showing how the page will look on a PC. Attraction is the main quality as most people make there mind up in the first 3 seconds to decide if they are going to read the content. Another Reason is that the easier your page is to use the more likely your viewers are to turn to customers. That's right its been studied that people are improving their conversation rates up to 300% by changing their sites responsiveness.

Website Responsiveness also is a advantage to anyone creating the page as their is no need to watch out for new uses and platforms you have already covered them all. If social media is one of your key traffic drivers then a responsive site will help as the traffic will be arriving from mobile devices. When a web page is 'mobile first' you can make sure you only deliver what the viewer needs to see.

A responsive web page will also reduce user confusion which is when a viewer questions if they are on the same companies page. Your web page will also make customers able to find you with feature to integrate google maps and to give the address information easily located. Finally I bring you the last reason for web responsiveness. If you choose to ignore catering for Tablet and Mobile friendly website you might as well not bother with your website. The need to cater for all devices is a must as many users are on mobile. You must NOT let your customers down and don't let your competitors get ahead.

Turn Your Website to an App on Android Devices!

by Jordan Abram

Posted on August 29, 2014 at 8:25 PM

Turning Your Website into a App is usually hard as you also need programming skills too. Which only means more time. Not anymore thanks to the wonderful developers at Google this has been made easier. I will explain what this feature is and how to do it.

This Feature is Unique to Google Chrome. Released in Chrome M31 Beta you can set your website to open in full-screen "app mode" using Chrome's "Add to homescreen" menu item. This feature is a big help especially from a development point of view. This can save time and money. You can also put it on Google Play Store. When you launch the app you will recieve full-screen with no vestiges of a browser. As seen below.

Now We begin to talk how can you do this amazing feature. You Would think it would be complex but suprisingly not. Firstly all you do is add a line into the top of your html. 

<meta name="mobile-web-app-capable" content="yes">
Then add one of these link tags into your html. 
<link rel="icon" sizes="192x192" href="nice-highres.png">(RECOMMENDED)

<linkrel="icon" sizes="128x128" href="nice-icon.png">

<link rel="apple-touch-icon" sizes="128x128"href="niceicon.png">

<link rel="apple-touch-icon-precomposed" sizes="128x128"href="niceicon.png">
Finally create the icon you wish to display as the icon. Mine is the one you see in the picture which is a quick one for the purpose of this video and then your done.

Adding the App to Homescreen

Firstly load up Google Chrome on your Android Device. The Navigate to the Chrome Menu Button which looks like this

Next you will see "Add to homepage" once you click that item and give it a name it will be on your Android Device Homescreen.
